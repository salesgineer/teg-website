# Inspection Parameter Specifications

**Date:** 2025-11-08  
**Status:** Research in Progress

## Parameter Definitions and Standards

### 1. Paint Thickness (Microns)

**What it measures:** Thickness of paint layers on vehicle body panels

**Measurement Method:** Paint thickness gauge (ultrasonic or magnetic)

**Normal Ranges:**
- **Factory Original:** 80-150 microns (varies by manufacturer)
- **European Cars:** 100-180 microns typical
- **Japanese Cars:** 80-120 microns typical
- **American Cars:** 100-150 microns typical

**Status Thresholds:**
- **Excellent (Green):** 80-180 microns, consistent across panels
- **Good (Green):** 80-200 microns, minor variations acceptable
- **Warning (Yellow):** <80 microns (thin, possible damage) OR >200 microns (repaint likely)
- **Critical (Red):** <60 microns (severe damage) OR >300 microns (major repaint/accident)

**What it indicates:**
- Values below normal = possible damage, rust repair, or poor repaint
- Values above normal = repaint, accident repair, or panel replacement
- Inconsistent values across panels = accident damage or repair work

**Industry Standard:** ISO 2808 (Paint thickness measurement)

### 2. Engine Compression (PSI)

**What it measures:** Pressure created in engine cylinders during compression stroke

**Measurement Method:** Compression tester connected to spark plug hole

**Normal Ranges (by engine type):**
- **4-Cylinder:** 120-180 PSI
- **6-Cylinder:** 130-190 PSI
- **8-Cylinder:** 140-200 PSI
- **Diesel:** 300-500 PSI (much higher)

**Status Thresholds:**
- **Excellent (Green):** Within 10 PSI of manufacturer spec, all cylinders within 10% of each other
- **Good (Green):** Within 15 PSI of spec, cylinders within 15% variation
- **Warning (Yellow):** 20-30 PSI below spec OR >20% variation between cylinders
- **Critical (Red):** >30 PSI below spec OR >30% variation (indicates engine problems)

**What it indicates:**
- Low compression = worn piston rings, valves, or head gasket issues
- Variation between cylinders = uneven wear or specific cylinder problems
- Consistent low values = general engine wear

**Industry Standard:** SAE J1349 (Engine performance standards)

---

### 3. Brake Pad Thickness (mm)

**What it measures:** Remaining material thickness on brake pads

**Measurement Method:** Visual inspection with caliper measurement

**Normal Ranges:**
- **New Pads:** 10-12 mm
- **Good Condition:** >6 mm
- **Acceptable:** 3-6 mm
- **Needs Replacement:** 1-3 mm
- **Critical:** <1 mm

**Status Thresholds:**
- **Excellent (Green):** >6 mm (plenty of life remaining)
- **Good (Green):** 3-6 mm (acceptable, monitor)
- **Warning (Yellow):** 1-3 mm (replace soon)
- **Critical (Red):** <1 mm (immediate replacement required, safety risk)

**What it indicates:**
- Thickness affects braking performance and safety
- Uneven wear = brake system problems (calipers, rotors)
- Very thin pads = metal-on-metal contact, rotor damage

**Industry Standard:** 
- EU: Minimum 1.5 mm legal requirement
- US: Varies by state, typically 1/8 inch (3.2 mm) minimum

---

### 4. Tire Tread Depth (mm)

**What it measures:** Depth of tire tread grooves

**Measurement Method:** Tread depth gauge or coin test

**Normal Ranges:**
- **New Tires:** 8-10 mm
- **Good Condition:** >4 mm
- **Acceptable:** 3-4 mm
- **Legal Minimum (EU):** 1.6 mm
- **Legal Minimum (US):** 2/32 inch (1.6 mm)

**Status Thresholds (EU/Latvia):**
- **Excellent (Green):** >4 mm (excellent grip)
- **Good (Green):** 3-4 mm (good condition)
- **Warning (Yellow):** 1.6-3 mm (legal but low, replace soon)
- **Critical (Red):** <1.6 mm (illegal in EU, unsafe, immediate replacement)

**What it indicates:**
- Affects grip, handling, and safety, especially in wet conditions
- Uneven wear = alignment, suspension, or inflation issues
- Very low tread = hydroplaning risk, longer braking distances

**Industry Standard (EU/Latvia):**
- **EU Regulation (EC 661/2009):** Minimum 1.6 mm legal requirement
- **Latvia:** Follows EU standards, 1.6 mm minimum
- **Winter Tires:** Some EU countries require 3-4 mm minimum for winter
- **CSDD (Latvia):** Enforces 1.6 mm minimum during technical inspection

---

### 5. Battery Voltage (Volts)

**What it measures:** Electrical charge level of car battery

**Measurement Method:** Multimeter across battery terminals (engine off)

**Normal Ranges:**
- **Fully Charged:** 12.6-12.8 V
- **Good Charge:** 12.4-12.6 V
- **Acceptable:** 12.2-12.4 V
- **Low Charge:** 12.0-12.2 V
- **Weak/Dead:** <12.0 V

**Status Thresholds:**
- **Excellent (Green):** 12.6-12.8 V (fully charged, optimal)
- **Good (Green):** 12.4-12.6 V (good charge)
- **Warning (Yellow):** 12.0-12.4 V (low charge, may need charging)
- **Critical (Red):** <12.0 V (weak/dead battery, may not start)

**What it indicates:**
- Battery health and ability to start vehicle
- Low voltage = old battery, charging system problems, or parasitic drain
- Should be tested with engine off (resting voltage)

**Industry Standard:** SAE J537 (Battery standards)

---

### 6. Coolant Temperature (°C)

**What it measures:** Operating temperature of engine coolant

**Measurement Method:** OBD-II scanner or temperature gauge

**Normal Ranges:**
- **Cold Start:** <40°C
- **Normal Operating:** 85-95°C (varies by vehicle)
- **Warming Up:** 40-85°C
- **Overheating:** >100°C

**Status Thresholds:**
- **Excellent (Green):** 85-95°C (optimal operating temperature)
- **Good (Green):** 80-100°C (acceptable range)
- **Warning (Yellow):** 70-80°C (cool, may indicate thermostat issue) OR 95-100°C (getting hot)
- **Critical (Red):** <70°C (too cool, thermostat stuck open) OR >100°C (overheating, engine damage risk)

**What it indicates:**
- Engine cooling system efficiency
- Too cool = thermostat problems, poor fuel economy
- Too hot = cooling system failure, risk of engine damage

**Industry Standard:** Typically 87-93°C for most modern vehicles

---

### 7. Oil Quality/Level

**What it measures:** Engine oil condition and quantity

**Measurement Method:** Visual inspection + dipstick level check

**Normal Ranges:**
- **Level:** Between min/max marks on dipstick
- **Color:** Golden/amber (new) to dark brown (used but OK)
- **Consistency:** Smooth, not gritty or milky

**Status Thresholds:**
- **Excellent (Green):** Full level, golden/amber color, smooth consistency
- **Good (Green):** Full level, dark brown but clean, smooth
- **Warning (Yellow):** Low level OR very dark/black OR slightly gritty
- **Critical (Red):** Very low/empty OR milky (water contamination) OR very gritty (engine wear)

**What it indicates:**
- Engine lubrication and health
- Low level = leaks or consumption
- Milky = head gasket failure (water in oil)
- Gritty = engine wear particles

**Industry Standard:** Check every 5,000-10,000 km or per manufacturer recommendation

---

### 8. Suspension Travel (mm)

**What it measures:** Maximum compression/extension of suspension components

**Measurement Method:** Visual inspection + bounce test, or specialized equipment

**Normal Ranges:**
- **Front Suspension:** 100-150 mm typical travel
- **Rear Suspension:** 120-180 mm typical travel
- **Varies by:** Vehicle type, suspension design (MacPherson, multi-link, etc.)

**Status Thresholds:**
- **Excellent (Green):** Full travel, smooth operation, no leaks
- **Good (Green):** Adequate travel, minor wear acceptable
- **Warning (Yellow):** Reduced travel OR visible leaks OR excessive bounce
- **Critical (Red):** Minimal/no travel OR severe leaks OR completely failed

**What it indicates:**
- Suspension system health and ride quality
- Reduced travel = worn shocks/struts, poor handling
- Leaks = damaged seals, loss of damping

**Industry Standard:** Varies significantly by vehicle type and suspension design

---

### 9. VIN History Score (0-100)

**What it measures:** Comprehensive vehicle history based on VIN check

**Measurement Method:** Database checks (CarVertical, AutoDNA, insurance records, etc.)

**Scoring Criteria:**
- **Accidents:** -20 points per major accident, -10 per minor
- **Ownership Changes:** -5 points per frequent change
- **Service History:** +10 points for complete records, -10 for missing
- **Mileage Consistency:** +10 if consistent, -20 if discrepancies
- **Title Issues:** -30 for salvage/rebuilt title

**Status Thresholds:**
- **Excellent (Green):** 80-100 (clean history, well-maintained)
- **Good (Green):** 60-79 (minor issues, acceptable)
- **Warning (Yellow):** 40-59 (some concerns, investigate further)
- **Critical (Red):** 0-39 (major issues, high risk)

**What it indicates:**
- Overall vehicle history and risk level
- High score = clean, well-maintained vehicle
- Low score = potential problems, accidents, or fraud

**Industry Standard:** Based on multiple database checks and cross-referencing

---

### 10. Mileage Consistency

**What it measures:** Verification that odometer reading matches service records and vehicle condition

**Measurement Method:** Compare odometer reading with:
- Service records
- VIN history databases
- Vehicle wear patterns
- Interior/exterior condition

**Normal Ranges:**
- **Consistent:** Odometer matches all records and condition
- **Minor Discrepancy:** ±5,000 km variation acceptable
- **Major Discrepancy:** >10,000 km difference

**Status Thresholds:**
- **Excellent (Green):** Perfect match across all sources
- **Good (Green):** Minor variations (±5,000 km), explainable
- **Warning (Yellow):** Moderate discrepancies (5,000-10,000 km)
- **Critical (Red):** Major discrepancies (>10,000 km) OR obvious fraud

**What it indicates:**
- Odometer tampering (fraud)
- Incomplete service records
- Vehicle condition vs. stated mileage mismatch

**Industry Standard:** EU Regulation requires odometer tampering to be reported

## Notes

- Ranges may vary by vehicle make, model, age, and region
- These are general guidelines - specific vehicles may have different norms
- Professional inspection should consider all factors together, not individually
- Some parameters require specialized equipment to measure accurately
